ü´Ä CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

Steps to perform CPR:

  1. Check for responsiveness - tap shoulders and shout
  2. Call 108 for emergency services
  3. Place heel of one hand on center of chest
  4. Place other hand on top, interlock fingers
  5. Push hard and fast (100-120 compressions per minute)
  6. Allow chest to fully recoil between compressions
  7. Continue until help arrives or person responds

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

Only perform CPR if the person is unconscious and not breathing normally. For children, use gentler compressions.

ü§ê Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)

Steps for choking relief:

  1. Ask "Are you choking?" - if they can speak, let them cough
  2. Stand behind the person, wrap arms around waist
  3. Make a fist with one hand, place thumb side above navel
  4. Grasp fist with other hand, give quick upward thrusts
  5. Continue until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious
  6. If unconscious, begin CPR

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

For pregnant women or obese persons, place hands higher on chest. For infants, use back blows and chest thrusts.

ü©∏ Severe Bleeding

Steps to control bleeding:

  1. Call 108 immediately for severe bleeding
  2. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth or bandage
  3. Elevate the injured area above heart level
  4. If bleeding continues, apply more pressure
  5. Do not remove objects embedded in wound
  6. Keep pressure until help arrives

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

Never remove embedded objects. If blood soaks through, add more layers without removing the first.

üî• Burns

Steps for burn treatment:

  1. Remove person from source of burn
  2. Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for 10-15 minutes
  3. Remove jewelry and tight clothing before swelling
  4. Cover with clean, dry cloth or bandage
  5. Do not use ice, butter, or other home remedies
  6. Seek medical help for severe burns

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

For chemical burns, flush with water for at least 20 minutes. For electrical burns, ensure power is off first.

ü¶¥ Fractures & Sprains

Steps for fracture/sprain care:

  1. Keep the injured area still and supported
  2. Apply ice wrapped in cloth for 15-20 minutes
  3. Elevate the injured area if possible
  4. Do not try to straighten or move the injured area
  5. Seek medical attention for suspected fractures
  6. Use RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

If bone is visible or limb is deformed, call 108 immediately. Do not attempt to reset the bone.

üå°Ô∏è Heat Stroke

Steps for heat stroke treatment:

  1. Move person to cool, shaded area
  2. Remove excess clothing
  3. Cool the person with water, ice packs, or wet cloths
  4. Fan the person to increase cooling
  5. Give cool water if person is conscious
  6. Call 108 if symptoms are severe

‚ö†Ô∏è Important:

Heat stroke is a medical emergency. Symptoms include high body temperature, confusion, and loss of consciousness.
